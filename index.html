Investor return table
Management economics (carry above hurdle)

IRR — annualized return on the investor's cash flows: initial capital out (Year 0), annual distributions in (Years 1–N), plus return of principal at end of horizon. Calculated via Newton-Raphson.  |  MOIC — total cash returned ÷ capital invested, measured over the selected horizon. Always specify the time period alongside MOIC — a 2× over 3 years is very different from 2× over 8 years.  |  DPI — distributions paid in only (excludes terminal principal return). Shows how much cash the investor has received before exit.  |  Capital payback — year when cumulative distributions alone (before terminal return) recover the initial investment.  |  Carry — management's share of profits above the hurdle rate, earned only after investor preferred return is fully satisfied each year.  |  Important: the investor's cost is already embedded in the blended cost of capital charged to the loan portfolio in the P&L. The investor tab shows how that cost is distributed as cash flows — it does not add an additional charge on top of the P&L.
